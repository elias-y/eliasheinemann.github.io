Solitary Waves in the Linear Shallow Water Equations

Posted on 2025-12-10

In this post we derive initial conditions that produce a pure right-traveling wave in the linear shallow-water equations

The equations

We consider the linear SWE system: 

$ h_t + \bar h\, v_x = 0, \quad v_t + g\, h_x = 0. $

The key observation is that the system diagonalizes under the Riemann invariants: 

$ w_{\pm} = v \pm \sqrt{g / \bar h}\, h. $

These satisfy transport equations 

$ w_{+t} + c w_{+x} = 0,\qquad w_{-t} - c w_{-x} = 0, $

meaning $w_+$ moves right and $w_-$ moves left.

Pure right-moving solutions

To kill the left-traveling mode, set: 

$ w_-(x,0) = 0 \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad v(x,0) = \sqrt{\frac{g}{\bar h}} h(x,0). $

This produces a solution of the form: 

$ h(x,t) = h(x - ct, 0),\qquad v(x,t) = v(x - ct, 0). $

Exactly the solitary traveling wave used in the assignment.
